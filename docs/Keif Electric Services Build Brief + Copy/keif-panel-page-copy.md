## 2. Signs you need a panel upgrade `[symptom-led opening captures the "is my panel a problem" searcher]`

You might need a service upgrade or panel replacement if:

- Your breakers trip regularly, or the panel feels warm or buzzes
- You have a fuse box or an outdated panel brand known for problems
- You're adding a big load — EV charger, heat pump, hot tub, AC, or a remodel
- Your insurance company or PG&E flagged your panel
- Your home still runs on a 100-amp service and you're out of room
- The panel is 25 to 30-plus years old and has never been touched

If a couple of those sound familiar, it's worth a look before it becomes a bigger problem.

## 3. What a panel upgrade costs in SLO County

Straight answer: a residential service upgrade in San Luis Obispo County typically runs **$3,500 to $5,500**, with most standard 200-amp upgrades landing in that range.

What moves the price:

- **Current vs. new service size** — 100-amp to 200-amp is the common jump
- **Whether the meter and mast need work** — sometimes the utility side needs upgrading too
- **Panel location and access** — a tight or awkward spot adds labor
- **Condition of existing wiring** — older homes sometimes need a few circuits brought up to code
- **Permit and utility coordination** — included in the quote, not tacked on later

You get a written quote before any work begins. If something unexpected turns up behind the wall, we talk about it before I touch it.

## 5. Permits, inspection, and PG&E

A service upgrade always requires a permit, and it usually involves coordinating with PG&E to disconnect and reconnect power. That's a lot of moving parts if you're doing it yourself — it's routine for me.

I pull the permit, schedule the utility disconnect and reconnect, coordinate the inspection, and make sure the new panel is signed off to code. Unpermitted electrical work can void your insurance and create headaches at resale, so this part isn't optional and I don't skip it.

## 8. Panel upgrade FAQ `[FAQPage schema on this block]`

**How much does a 200-amp panel upgrade cost?**
Most residential service upgrades in SLO County run $3,500 to $5,500 depending on your current service, wiring condition, and whether the meter or mast needs work. You get a written quote before anything starts.

**Do I need a permit to upgrade my electrical panel?**
Yes. Service upgrades require a permit and usually a PG&E disconnect and reconnect. I handle the permit, utility coordination, and inspection.

**How long does a panel upgrade take?**
Most standard upgrades are a one-day job, though power is off for part of it and the utility coordination can affect scheduling. I'll give you a realistic timeline with the quote.

**Do I need to upgrade my panel to install an EV charger?**
Sometimes. Older 100-amp panels often can't handle a Level 2 charger's load. I check capacity before quoting, and if an upgrade is needed I'll quote both together.

**What's the difference between a panel upgrade and a panel replacement?**
An upgrade increases your service capacity — for example, 100 amps to 200 amps. A replacement swaps an old or unsafe panel for a modern, code-compliant one at the same capacity. Some jobs are both. I'll tell you which yours is.

**What areas do you serve for panel upgrades?**
San Luis Obispo, Atascadero, Paso Robles, and surrounding SLO County. Not sure I reach you? Call and ask.
